Chowmein

Chowmein is a popular stir-fried noodle dish that originated in Chinese cuisine but has become a favorite around the world, especially in South Asia. It is made by tossing boiled noodles with vegetables like cabbage, carrots, and bell peppers, along with sauces such as soy sauce, chili sauce, and vinegar for a tangy, savory flavor. Often, chicken, beef, or shrimp is added to make it more filling. The dish is cooked on high heat to give it a slightly smoky taste and a satisfying texture. Chowmein is commonly enjoyed as a main course or street food, loved for its quick preparation and bold flavors.
